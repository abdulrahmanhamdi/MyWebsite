About This Course

This course offers a comprehensive introduction to the core principles and strategies used in algorithm design and analysis. Starting from fundamental data structures like arrays and stacks, students are gradually introduced to essential concepts such as recursion, iteration, and efficiency analysis.

Through detailed topics like Brute Force methods, Recursive and Non-Recursive algorithms, and Asymptotic Analysis, the course builds a strong analytical foundation. It then advances to classic algorithmic strategies including Decrease-and-Conquer, Divide-and-Conquer (e.g., Quicksort, Karatsuba), and Transform-and-Conquer techniques.

Modern approaches such as Dynamic Programming and Greedy Techniques are also covered, emphasizing their application in optimization and problem-solving. Additional focus is placed on time and space trade-offs, input enhancement strategies, and advanced structures like B-Trees.
